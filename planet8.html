Planet 8

CFBDSIR 2149−0403


An image seen in infrared

Mass

4 to 7 times that of Jupiter

Radius

Unknown (for now!)

Distance Away/Where They Are

100 light-years away, within the AB Doradus Moving group

Physical Descriptors

CFBDSIR 2149−0403 is estimated to be 50-120 million years old, and has a temperature of 400 degrees Celcius

Interesting Properties/Factoids

Scientists are not certain whether CFBDSIR 2149−0403 is a rogue planet or a brown dwarf, due to its location within a tightly-knit, moving group of stars.

How It Was Discovered

CFBDSIR 2149−0403 was discovered by scientists in Hawaii and Chile, using two infrared telescopes, the Canada-France-Hawaii Telescope and the Very Large Telescope, designed to look for young planets. It was discovered in an area of space 1,000 times that of the moon, and was the only one of its kind found within that area.
