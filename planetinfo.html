Information about Rogue Planets

Scroll down to learn more about rogue planets!

Rogue planets, because they aren't tied gravitationally to a star, are both more easily studied and less easily observed, as their light isn't inhibited by nearby starlight, but they have faint heat signatures that are hard to detect and distinguish from brown dwarfs and other tiny stellar objects. Rogue planets, due to their planet-like size and nature, may provide clues into how gas giants like Jupiter and Saturn formed
