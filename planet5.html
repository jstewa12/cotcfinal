Age

200 million years old

13 times that of Jupiter

Radius

At least 1.01 times that of Jupiter

Distance Away/Where They Are

6 parsecs away, in the Carina-Near moving group

Interesting Properties/Factoids

It was first thought that SIMP J013656.5+093347 was a brown dwarf, but it was proven in 2017 that it was more planet-like than scientists had observed. The object also has weather, making its discovery and new classification particularly interesting.

How It Was Discovered

Similarly to SDSS J111010.01+011613.1, SIMP J013656.5+093347 was discovered during the BASS-Ultracool survey, which was dedicated to finding and observing rogue planets and similarly-massed objects using the BANYAN 2 tool.
