The location of PSO J318.5-22 in space

Age

12 million years old

Mass

6 times that of Jupiter

Radius

Around the same as Jupiter

Distance Away/Where They Are

80 light-years away, located in Beat Pictoris moving group within Capricornus

Interesting Properties/Factoids

PSO J318.5-22 is the brighest rogue planet we can see, one of the least massive free-floating objects, and the first planet discovered outside of our solar system to have weather -- consisting of iron rain! Also, a day on PSO J318.5-22 would be only 5 hours!

How It Was Discovered

PSO J318.5-22 was discovered using the PS1 telescope, a telescope designed to search for brown dwarf stars, which are extremely hard to detect because of their low temperatures, by scanning for their radiation signals. The discovery of PSO J318.5-22 and it's classification as a rogue planet were confirmed by infrared telescopes in Hawaii.
