How It Was Discovered

SDSS J111010.01+011613.1 was discovered as part of the BAT AGN Spectroscopic Survey (BASS), a survey dedicated to carrying out a one-of-a-kind survey of radiation data from the night sky. An extension of the survey, aimed at detecting brown dwarfs, utilized a BANYAN 2 tool that found the probability of a certain object being part of a certain moving group of stars. Due to SDSS J111010.01+011613.1's size, it is classified as a rogue planet
